01 — Context

The right problem to solve.

Back offices and treasurers at Crédit Agricole CIB were managing daily cash positions across multiple disconnected apps, Excel macros, swift messages, and phone calls. Data wasn't reliable.

The existing tool had been built feature by feature, sprint by sprint, without a designer. It worked technically — but created more friction than it removed.

"A single data error has immediate financial consequences. Clarity isn't a UX principle — it's a risk management requirement."
0→1B2B FintechFirst DesignerLow Agile Maturity
02 — Research

One full day of shadowing. Everything changed.

Before sketching anything, I spent a full day embedded with back-office teams — observing real workflows, not assumed ones. That day surfaced unformulated needs and invisible workarounds that never appeared in specs.

Then: co-design workshops with 6 users, the PO and 2 BAs — 3 hours in person. Users became co-authors of the product, not passive recipients.

Shadowing output — experience map + opportunities workshop

03 — Design

Reconciliation is where complexity lives.

The hardest use case: reconciliation — matching bank transactions so the cash balance reflects the actual account. Pre-filtered data, accessible table creation, quick-view of daily positions.

Both light and dark modes delivered to match different trading floor environments. Developers integrated from day one — not at handoff.

Platform architecture — cash flow overview & reconciliation

Reconciliation screen — dark mode

04 — Impact

The platform is in production.

By consolidating cash flows into a single interface, the project eliminated the need to juggle between multiple applications and manually recalculate data in Excel.

Beyond the product: a design/business collaboration dynamic installed from scratch — regular workshops, shared roadmap, a team that now understands the value of UX in a high-criticality context.

Three wins: design/business trust established. User testing normalized. Systematic collaboration with clear rituals and shared ownership.
